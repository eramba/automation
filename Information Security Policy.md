## Objectives
The objectives of this policy are to:

- Establish a structured approach to information security management  
- Protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets  
- Ensure compliance with applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations  
- Support continual improvement of the Information Security Management System (ISMS)

## Organizational Security Objectives
Information security objectives shall be defined, reviewed, and approved by management and aligned with business and compliance requirements.  
Each objective must be measurable and periodically evaluated to ensure effectiveness.

Examples of security objectives include:

- Reducing the number of information security incidents  
- Ensuring systems maintain agreed levels of availability  
- Maintaining compliance with ISO/IEC 27001 and related frameworks

## Leadership Commitment
Top management shall demonstrate leadership and commitment to the ISMS by:

- Ensuring the integration of information security requirements into business processes  
- Providing necessary resources and support for achieving information security objectives  
- Communicating the importance of effective information security management  
- Promoting continual improvement of the ISMS

## Leadership Oversight & Review
Management shall review the ISMS at planned intervals to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness.  
The review shall include:

- Progress toward achieving information security objectives  
- Results from audits, monitoring, and performance metrics  
- Nonconformities and corrective actions  
- Opportunities for improvement  

Review outcomes shall include decisions and actions related to updates to policies, objectives, or resources.

## Roles, Responsibilities & Skills
Responsibilities for information security are defined and communicated to ensure accountability at all levels.

- The Information Security Officer or equivalent role is responsible for the implementation and maintenance of the ISMS  
- Department heads are responsible for enforcing controls within their areas  
- All employees and contractors must adhere to security policies and report incidents  

Personnel involved in security activities shall possess appropriate skills and training.
